A trimtab is the small rudder that moves the big rudder that turns the whole ship. R. Buckminster Fuller named it, wore the word on his gravestone, made it a philosophy of intervention: you don't attack the hull, you don't wrestle the rudder, you find the tiny surface that everything else pivots on and you move that. Bret absorbed Fuller's physics and extended them into the specific grammar of the Syntony Times universe, where the trimtab names not just a strategy but a posture — a way of being in relationship to large, slow, apparently immovable systems.

In the Syntony Times corpus, the term appears as early as April 2023, when "Put It On Our Trimtab: Mini Togetherland Treatment" named Togetherland — the vision of a co-created living experiment — as exactly this kind of lever: not a frontal assault on broken systems but a demonstration so coherent that the surrounding world has to recalibrate around it. The 2024 treatment by the same title extended that frame, moving Trimtab from metaphor into production logic: what gets built, how it gets built, and in what sequence.

The fullest articulation in the corpus comes in "Bitcoin → Ethereum → Storyliving" (August 2025), where Bret names perception as the smallest lever with the largest reach — the trimtab of civilization itself. Not the ledger, not the law, not the institution. The story. Change the story people are living inside and the systems those stories generate change naturally; leave the story intact and you can rebuild every structure and watch the old myths reassert themselves through the new architecture. This is why crypto, in his reading, failed as a trimtab: it thought the ledger was the lever when the lever was always the narrative underneath the ledger.

In the undated "The Department of Livingry Radical," Radical Philanthropy itself is named a trimtab — the tiny act of unconditional generosity that demonstrates a different economy is possible before that economy has any institutions to protect it.
